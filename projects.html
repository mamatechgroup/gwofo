About This Project

Our Girls Education Program addresses the critical barriers to education faced by young girls in Liberia. Through comprehensive support systems, we ensure that girls not only enroll in school but also complete their education successfully.

Key Objectives:

  • Provide scholarships for 500 girls annually
  • Distribute school supplies to 1,200 girls
  • Establish mentorship programs in 15 schools
  • Conduct parent awareness workshops
  • Improve school infrastructure in 5 communities

Impact So Far:

Since inception, we have supported over 5,000 girls, resulting in a 40% increase in girls' school retention rates in target communities. 85% of scholarship recipients have progressed to higher education or vocational training.

About This Project

This initiative empowers women in rural Liberia through business skills training, micro-finance support, and market access facilitation, creating sustainable livelihoods and boosting local economies.

Key Components:

  • Business management training
  • Micro-loans and financial literacy
  • Market linkage development
  • Mentorship from successful women entrepreneurs
  • Cooperative formation support

Success Stories:

Over 200 women have successfully started or expanded their businesses, creating employment opportunities for an additional 500 community members. Average household income has increased by 45% among participants.

About This Project

This completed project provided essential healthcare services to women and girls in remote areas through mobile clinics, health education, and community health worker training.

Achievements:

  • Established 5 mobile clinic units
  • Trained 50 community health workers
  • Conducted 120 health education workshops
  • Provided prenatal care to 800 women
  • Distributed 5,000 health kits

Impact Assessment:

The project achieved a 60% reduction in maternal complications and a 40% increase in immunization rates in target communities. Community health workers continue to provide services using skills gained from the project.
